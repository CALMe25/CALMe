How directive should the bot be during crisis?

Each option shows a simulated conversation with someone sheltering during a rocket alert

A

Firm & Specific — First Responder Voice

I'm here with you. What's your name?
Dana
Dana, count the people around you right now. Tell me the number.
4 people
Good. Look at each person. Point to the one who seems most stressed. Go check on them now. Ask if they need water.
there's a kid crying
Go to the child. Kneel to their level. Say: "My name is Dana. I'm going to stay right here with you." Do that now.

Strengths

  • Clear, no ambiguity
  • Activates prefrontal cortex immediately
  • Matches Six Cs "Challenge" precisely
  • Works when person can barely think

Risks

  • Could feel bossy or cold
  • No room for "I can't do that"
  • Same tone whether calm or panicking
B

Guided & Gentle — Supportive Friend Voice

Hey, I'm here. What should I call you?
Dana
Dana, can you look around? Who do you see near you?
some people, a kid
Is anyone looking like they could use some support? Sometimes even a small thing helps.
the kid is crying
That sounds hard. Do you think you could go sit near them? Sometimes just being close helps a kid feel safer.

Strengths

  • Warm, non-threatening
  • User has choice — feels in control
  • Works for lower-stress situations

Risks

  • Too vague when someone is panicking
  • "Can you...?" invites "no"
  • Doesn't fully activate executive function
  • Slower to restore agency
C

Adaptive — Reads Stress Level, Shifts Tone

HIGH STRESS DETECTED — firm mode
I'm here. I'm not going anywhere. What's your name?
dana i can't breathe
Dana. Press your feet into the floor. Feel that? Good. Now count the people near you. How many?
4 i think. there's a kid crying
4 people, one kid. Go to the child now. Kneel down and tell them your name.
ok i did that. she stopped crying a little
STABILIZING — shifting to guided mode
You did that. You helped her. Does anyone else around you look like they could use something? Water, a blanket?

Strengths

  • Firm when needed, gentle when possible
  • Matches Six Cs model precisely — directive to restore function, then softer
  • Acknowledges progress ("You did that")
  • Transitions feel natural

Risks

  • More complex to implement
  • Stress detection must be accurate
