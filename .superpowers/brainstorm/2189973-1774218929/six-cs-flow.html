2. The Six Cs Sequence — Adaptive, Skippable

Commitment
→
Cognitive
→
Challenge
→
Control
→

Compassion woven through every message
Commitment "I'm here. I'm not going anywhere." ~5 sec
Cognitive "Count the people around you." / "What floor are you on?" / "How many exits can you see?" ~15 sec
Challenge "Go check on the person who looks most stressed." / "Find water for the child." / "Ask the person next to you their name." ~30 sec
Control "Do you want to get them water, or sit with them?" / "Would you rather check on the elderly person or the child first?" ~15 sec
Continuity "The siren went off at [time]. You got to shelter. You've helped [person]. The threat will pass." ~20 sec

Skip logic: If user says "I'm with 6 people and a kid is freaking out" → skip Commitment + Cognitive, jump to Challenge.
Fallback: When stress level is unknown, default to firm/directive (option A).

3. Inline Micro-Activities vs Sidebar Activities

During Crisis (inline only)

  • "Count the people around you"
  • "Describe 3 things you can see"
  • "Check if anyone needs water"
  • "Ask the person next to you their name"
  • "What supplies do you have?"
  • "Is anyone injured?"

Activities sidebar HIDDEN. No breathing exercises. No games. Stay in the real world.

After Crisis (sidebar available)

  • Breathing exercises
  • Matching game
  • Digital canvas
  • Snake, Sudoku, puzzles
  • Stretching routine

Activities sidebar VISIBLE. Recovery mode. Decompression. Calming is appropriate now.

4. Adaptive Tone — Firm → Guided Transition

CRISIS — FIRM MODE
I'm here with you. What's your name?
dana im so scared
Dana. Press your feet into the floor. Feel that? Now count the people near you.
4 people and a kid
Go to the child. Kneel down. Say your name. Do it now.
i did. she says her name is maya
You did that, Dana. Maya knows your name now. That matters.
STABILIZING — SHIFTING TO GUIDED
Does Maya need anything? Water, a blanket? What about the others around you?
i gave her water. theres an old man who looks shaky
You're helping people, Dana. Want to check on him too?
ALL CLEAR — RECOVERY MODE
The alert has passed. You made it — and you helped Maya and the others get through it too. How are you feeling?

Notice: short commanding sentences during crisis → longer supportive questions during recovery.
Compassion throughout: "You did that." / "That matters." / "You're helping people."
